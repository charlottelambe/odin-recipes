Red Lentil Dahl

This easy Indian Red Lentil Dahl is a must-try if you’re looking for a flavourful curry, soup or stew recipe. It’s delicious, healthy and one of the best lentil dishes to warm you up in winter.

Prep Time: 5 mins

Cook Time: 20 mins

Serves 4

Ingredients

For the Dahl:

  • 1-2 tbsp coconut oil for frying (or other neutral oil)
  • 2 onions chopped
  • 4 garlic cloves minced
  • 2 tsp ginger minced
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp coriander
  • 1 tsp red paprika powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala or other curry powder to taste
  • 300g red lentils
  • 780ml vegetable stock
  • 240ml coconut milk
  • 240ml strained tomatoes or chopped tomatoes
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tsp coconut sugar or to taste
  • 2-3 tbsp lime or lemon juice or to taste

To serve (optional):

  • 4-6 tbsp non-dairy yoghurt
  • Fresh parsley or coriander
  • Sesame seeds
  • Rice cooked

Method

  1. Heat the coconut oil in a skillet or saucepan. Add the chopped onions and sauté for another minute until fragrant. Lastly, add the spices and sauté for a few seconds to unfold flavours.
  2. Place the red lentils into a fine-meshed sieve and the rinse under cold running water.
  3. Add the washed red lentils to the onion mixture in the skillet. Pour in the vegetable stock, stir to combine, and bring to a simmer. Cook for 8-10 minutes, or until the lentils have absorbed most of the liquid.
  4. Add the coconut milk and strained tomatoes (if using) and simmer for another 5-10 minutes, or until the lentils are tender. If the sauce is too thick add a little coconut milk or vegetable stock, until the desired consistency is reached.
  5. Season with salt, pepper, coconut sugar and lime juice to taste.
  6. Garnish your dahl with a swirl of non-dairy yoghurt, fresh chopped parsley or coriander, and sesame seeds, or any other toppings you would like. Serve over rice or with a homemade naan.

Notes

Storage and freezing: Store leftover dahl in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3-5 days or freeze for future use.
