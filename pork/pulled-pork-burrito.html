Pulled Pork Burrito

If you love pulled pork, and you love burritos, you'll love these pulled pork burritos!

Prep Time: 30 mins

Cook Time: 12hrs plus 2hrs additional time

Serves 4

Ingredients

    To make the pork rub:

  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon chilli powder
  • 2 teaspoons paprika (sweet or smoked)
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • ½ teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon black pepper

    To make the pork:

  • 1 Bone-In Pork Shoulder, 6-8 lbs preferred
  • 2 tbsp Avocado Oil / Olive Oil / Mustard, for binding rub to meat
  • 4 tbsp Pork Rub

    For the burrito:

  • 1 lb pulled pork
  • 8 large flour tortillas
  • 1/2 cup shredded cheese
  • 1/2 cup black beans
  • 1/2 cup pico de gallo

Method

  1. Make the rub by combining the brown sugar chilli powder, paprika, garlic powder, thyme, onion powder, salt and pepper in a bowl and mix well.
  2. Using your hands, apply the binding agent to the exterior of the pork shoulder.
  3. Generously apply the rub pork shoulder with rub (4 tbsp of rub).
  4. Smoke Pork Shoulder at 225 degrees, after about 6-8 hours the internal temp should hit 160.
  5. When the internal temp hits 160 wrap the pork shoulder with butcher paper, and continue smoking. Check the internal temperature every hour, and when the internal temp hits 200+, pull it from the smoker.
  6. Wrap the pork shoulder with foil, and put in a cooler to rest. I like to let my pork shoulders rest for a minimum of two hours, up to five.
  7. Make the burrito by adding some pulled pork, cheese, black beans, and pico de gallo to the tortilla and then roll up the burrito.
  8. Place the burrito seam side down on your warm cast iron skillet to seal the seam.

Tips

  • If you don’t have a smoker, you could use a slow cooker or a normal fan oven to cook the pork.
  • For a more filling burrito you can add some spicy rice or some more vegetables.
  • An alternative rub is 2-1-1-1 ratio of brown sugar, paprika, kosher salt, and fresh ground black pepper
