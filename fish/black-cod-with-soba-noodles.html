Black Cod with Soba Noodles

If you’re looking for an easy Japanese dish to make at home, look no further. We’ve paired sticky sweet teriyaki glazed cod with a tasty ginger and sesame dressed noodle salad.

Serves 4

Ingredients

For the fish:

  • 4 fillets black cod or other white-fleshed fish
  • ¼ cup mayonnaise or Vegenaise
  • 2 tablespoons teriyaki sauce
  • 1 teaspoon wasabi paste

For the salad:

  • Salt
  • ½ pound soba noodles or whole wheat spaghetti
  • ¼ cup teriyaki sauce
  • ¼ cup honey
  • ¼ cup vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons sherry vinegar
  • 1 fresh red chilli pepper, seeded and chopped
  • 1 piece fresh ginger (1 1/2 to 2 inches), grated
  • 2 large cloves garlic, grated
  • Sesame oil, for drizzling
  • 2 cups napa cabbage, shredded
  • 1 cup thawed frozen shelled edamame
  • 5 - 6 spring onions, thinly sliced on an angle
  • 3 tablespoons sesame seeds, toasted

Method

  1. Bring a pot of water to a boil, salt it, add the pasta and cook until al dente. Drain, then rinse with cold water.
  2. While the pasta is working, position a rack in the upper third of the oven and preheat the broiler. In a bowl, whisk together 1/4 cup teriyaki, the honey, oil, vinegar, chile, ginger and garlic; drizzle in sesame oil to taste. Toss in the pasta, cabbage, edamame and scallions and top with the sesame seeds.
  3. Arrange the fish on a lightly greased baking sheet or broiler pan. In a small bowl, combine the mayo (or Vegenaise), 2 tbsp. teriyaki and the wasabi paste. Slather the sauce over the fish. Broil until firm, opaque and well browned, 8 to 10 minutes.
  4. Serve the fish on top of the pasta. Flake the fish to combine it with the pasta as you eat.
